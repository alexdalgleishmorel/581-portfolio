A Tale of Two Buttons

Description

This project describes our group member Carson Bergen. He is someone who greatly enjoys several different hobbies, the most notable of which being music and programming. On top of Carson's hobbies, he also greatly values taking care of his body, in which working-out and eating are essential aspects of his life.

For our project, we wanted to capture all these different aspects of Carson, since no single aspect was more important to him than another. As a result, we came up with the idea of creating quadrants, each of which represents a different portion of his hobbies or priorities. To demonstrate how he constantly transitions between these different interests, we created a figure representative of Carson, which can be dragged and move to different quadrants to allow him to do that activity.

Once Carson's figure has been dragged into a quadrant, an action button allows the user to make him do whichever activity the user has assigned him to. This way of demonstrating the different aspects of who Carson is allows the user to engage in an interactive experience where they must interact with Carson himself to truly reveal all the different elements of his personality.

Final implementation

Our final implementation of the project involved 4 quadrants. Each quadrant rotated between 3 different states. The first being that Carson's figure is not present, the second being that the figure is present but not animating, and the last being an animated version of the figure.

Division of work

The work for this was delegated amoungst the 3 of us in the following way: Guilherme was responsible for drawing all images and animations, while myself and Carson handled developing the design and functionality of the app where these images live. In terms of development work, I handled creating the quadrants and the general data context shared between all components. Using this data context, Carson then implemented the draggable figure and the clickable animation button.

How does our application fit the objective?

The initial goal of this project was to capture the essence of a team member's personality, with the limitation of interacting with just 2 buttons. In this implementation, we've achieved a medium of demonstrating an all-encompassing display of Carson's personality, without having to sacrifice important aspects of his self.
Beyond displaying many different aspects, the 2 buttons (the draggable character and the animation button), provide an interaction that is inviting to the user, and beckons people to interact with the interface. Upon interaction, Carson's self is immediately revealed and it makes the user want to see each aspect based on the entertaining animation.

What worked and what didn't work

One aspect that worked quite well with this implementation was the idea of compartmentalized sectors, but with the ability to transcend them as the core interaction. This provided a way of navigating several different topics while maintaining an overarching view that was easy to keep track of. The figure also snapping into place for the user helped to ensure that the state of the interface never appeared too cluttered.

An aspect that didn't quite work was that both buttons were based on the mouse's location. One button was draggable, thus always tracking the mouses location. Similarly, the animation button tracked the mouse as it moved. As a result, this left both interactions in close proximity to each other, sometimes resulting in a cluttered display where it may be tricky for the user to quickly interact with one button without potentially pressing the other.

One way of ammending this issue would be to use a piecewise-button approach like in several of our initial and refined sketches. The animation button could be placed in the center of the screen, intersecting all 4 quadrants. This way, its location would always be easily accessible to the user, which would allow them to better discern the two interactions of the app.
